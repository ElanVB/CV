Academic Publication Experience

Reviewer Experience

I have served as a reviewer for a number of prestigious machine learning conferences / journals. I reviewed a number of papers submitted to the international conference on machine learning (ICML) in 2019 relating to foundational theory of machine learning. The papers I reviewed covered topics such as: the theory of generalisation and optimisation, new regularisation methods, neural network initialisation, and the theory of activation functions.

I reviewed a number of papers submitted to the conference on neural information processing systems (NeurIPS) in 2019 relating to reinforcement learning and neural network initialisation. These papers discussed the pruning of neural networks, how information flows through neural networks, new initialisation techniques, and variational approaches in reinforcement learning.

In 2020 I reviewed papers for ICML again. Some of these papers also covered how information flows through neural networks, including more complex architectures such as transformers. However, the bulk of the papers I reviewed looked interactions between multiple reinforcement learning agents. focuses of these works were credit assignment / attribution methods, communication schemes, and value functions.

I was ranked in the top 400 reviewers, top 33% of, and 5% of reviewers for these conferences.
